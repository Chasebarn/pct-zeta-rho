Thousands of business majors graduate every year holding the same diploma. The ones who get the offers built their edge early — the skills, the reps, and the people. That’s what Phi Chi Theta is for.

Phi Chi Theta runs on three pillars. Each one hands you different skills, different ways to lead, and different people who’ve already been where you’re trying to go.

Professional. Get sharp before it counts.

Structured reps, semester after semester: resume and LinkedIn development, public speaking practice, workshops, guest speakers, and events like our Fall Summit — so the first time you’re in a room that matters, it isn’t your first time in that room.

Skills you take

Interview presence, articulation, and a resume that reads like you’ve done things — because you have.

Ways to lead

Help plan and run the chapter’s professional programming — speakers, workshops, and the Fall Summit itself.

People you meet

Mentorship from members and alumni already working in the roles you’re aiming for.

Philanthropy. Lead something real.

This is where leadership stops being a word on your resume. Members plan and run fundraising and service events for partners like the Hillsborough Education Foundation, JA Finance Park, and Parkland Cares — over $10,000 raised for the Tampa Bay community so far.

Skills you take

Planning, budgeting, and running an event end to end — project management you can describe in an interview with numbers attached.

Ways to lead

Own an initiative: pitch it, organize it, lead members through it, and measure what it raised.

People you meet

Community partners across Tampa Bay — and a chapter that shows up next to you on a Saturday morning.

Social. Friends for life — and a network for longer.

Intramural teams, our Fall Formal, the Spring Retreat, and everything in between. You’re building real friendships with people who share your interests — and those same people are the ones who help you find your way through college, and long after it.

What it feels like

Walking into a business class, an interview lobby, or a new city and already knowing someone.

Ways to lead

Run traditions — formals, retreats, intramurals — and shape the culture the next class inherits.

People you meet

Friends with your goals in mind. The kind who send you the internship posting before it’s public.

Questions? Answered.

Do I need to be a business major?

Rush is open to business and economics majors and minors. If business is part of your academic path at USF, you’re eligible.

I’m a freshman / senior — is it too early or too late?

Neither. Rush is open to all class years, and every class brings something different to the chapter.

Is rush a high-pressure evaluation?

No. Our process is built on mutual fit — six structured events where you get to know us as honestly as we get to know you. No prior experience with Greek or professional organizations is required.

What do I actually get out of rushing?

Resume and LinkedIn development, networking with business professionals, and public speaking practice — skills that stay with you whether or not you join.

Phi Chi ThetaZeta Rho · USF

Phi Chi Theta’s Zeta Rho Chapter was established at the University of South Florida in September 2018 by 18 founding business students. Since its founding, the chapter has focused on preparing members for internships and full-time roles through structured professional development, service, and chapter standards.
